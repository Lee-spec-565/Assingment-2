Niagara Falls State Park

Niagara Falls State Park is the oldest state park in the United States and home to one of the world's most breathtaking natural wonders. Visitors can experience the power of the American, Bridal Veil, and Horseshoe Falls up close through scenic overlooks, walking trails, and the iconic Maid of the Mist boat tour.
Enjoy panoramic viewpoints, lush green spaces, and stunning evening illuminations that make the falls shine day or night. With easy-to-access pathways, photo-perfect spots, and engaging attractions, Niagara Falls State Park offers an unforgettable adventure for every traveler.

Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island

The Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island are iconic landmarks in New York Harbor, representing freedom, hope, and the rich immigrant history of the United States. Visitors can explore the majestic Statue of Liberty, a gift from France, and learn about its significance through informative exhibits and displays that celebrate liberty and democracy.
Take a ferry ride to Liberty Island, visit the Statue of Liberty Museum, and climb to the crown for breathtaking views of the city skyline and harbor. On Ellis Island, wander through the National Museum of Immigration to discover interactive exhibits, personal stories, and historical artifacts that illustrate the journeys of millions of immigrants who helped shape America. This experience combines education, history, and stunning views, offering an unforgettable visit for travelers of all ages.

Central Park

Central Park is Manhattan's iconic green oasis, offering scenic trails, lakes, gardens, and world-famous landmarks like Bethesda Terrace, the Great Lawn, and Strawberry Fields. It provides a peaceful retreat from the bustling city streets.
Enjoy walking, biking, boating, picnics, or winter ice skating, and visit the Central Park Zoo. The park blends natural beauty, art, and recreation to create a memorable outdoor experience for visitors of all ages.

Empire State Building and Rockefeller Center

These legendary New York City skyscrapers are architectural marvels that dominate the Manhattan skyline. Both offer iconic observation decks with panoramic views, showcasing the city from above and providing perfect photo opportunities.
Experience dazzling city lights at night, seasonal displays, or special events from the Empire State Building. Enjoy the Rockefeller Plaza’s ice skating, holiday celebrations, and Top of the Rock observation deck for one of the best vantage points in New York.

Times Square

Times Square is New York’s vibrant entertainment district, famous for its neon lights, massive digital billboards, Broadway theaters, and nonstop energy. It’s often called “The Crossroads of the World.”
Take in a Broadway show, witness the New Year's Eve ball drop, shop for souvenirs, and be immersed in the electrifying atmosphere that makes Times Square a must-see destination for visitors.

Adirondack Park

Adirondack Park is a vast natural paradise covering over six million acres in upstate New York, filled with mountains, forests, rivers, and hundreds of lakes. It offers a perfect escape into nature.
Hike scenic trails, paddle or fish on peaceful lakes, ski in winter, and stay in cozy cabins or lodges. Adirondack Park is ideal for outdoor adventures, wildlife spotting, and reconnecting with nature year-round.

Letchworth State Park

Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” Letchworth State Park is famous for its dramatic gorge, cliffs, and three majestic waterfalls along the Genesee River. It combines natural beauty with recreational opportunities.
Explore scenic overlooks, hike woodland trails, enjoy whitewater rafting, or picnic along the riverbanks. Letchworth offers stunning landscapes that attract photographers, nature lovers, and families alike.

Watkins Glen State Park

Watkins Glen State Park in the Finger Lakes region is renowned for its picturesque gorge, 19 waterfalls, and striking rock formations along the main trail. The park showcases the natural beauty of upstate New York.
Walk the gorge trail, cross stone bridges, and marvel at cascading waterfalls. It’s a perfect destination for hikers, photographers, and families seeking scenic outdoor adventures.

Corning Museum of Glass

The Corning Museum of Glass offers an engaging journey through the history, art, and science of glassmaking from ancient times to modern innovations. It’s a unique blend of education and creativity.
Watch live glassblowing demonstrations, explore interactive exhibits, and participate in hands-on workshops to create your own glass keepsake, making it an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages.

Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown

The National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum celebrates the legends, history, and culture of America’s favorite pastime. It’s a tribute to the sport and its players.
Explore historic memorabilia, learn about iconic athletes, and enjoy interactive exhibits. Baseball fans and families alike can experience the rich legacy of the game firsthand.

LEGOLAND New York Resort

LEGOLAND New York Resort is a family-friendly theme park in the Hudson Valley, offering LEGO-themed rides, attractions, and interactive play areas. It’s designed for fun and imagination.
Build and play with LEGO bricks, ride roller coasters, meet favorite characters, and enjoy hands-on activities in a colorful, engaging environment for kids and families.

The Wild Center

The Wild Center in the Adirondacks immerses visitors in the region’s wildlife and natural environment through indoor and outdoor exhibits. It’s a hands-on nature experience.
Walk the treetop canopy, see river otters, join guided nature walks, and explore interactive exhibits. The center combines education, adventure, and spectacular views of the Adirondacks.

New York State Capitol in Albany

The New York State Capitol is an architectural masterpiece in Albany, blending historic grandeur with political significance. Visitors can explore its ornate interiors and rich history.
Take guided tours of the chambers, admire intricate stonework and artwork, and learn about New York State’s government and legislative history in an inspiring setting.

Saratoga Spa State Park

Saratoga Spa State Park offers a combination of natural beauty, mineral springs, recreation, and cultural attractions. It’s a relaxing and enriching destination for all visitors.
Enjoy mineral baths, outdoor pools, golf, hiking trails, and summer concerts. The park’s serene environment and cultural activities make it a perfect retreat for families and tourists alike.

Boldt Castle in Thousand Islands

Boldt Castle, set on Heart Island in the Thousand Islands, is an early 20th-century romantic castle with stunning views of the St. Lawrence River. It’s a picturesque historic landmark.
Tour the restored rooms, stroll through formal gardens, and enjoy the scenic waterfront. Boldt Castle offers history, romance, and breathtaking scenery for visitors of all ages.

History

History of New York

New York’s history is a fascinating journey that gives every landmark a deeper meaning. Before it became a world-famous destination, the region was home to Native American communities such as the Lenape, who lived along its rivers and forests. In 1624, Dutch settlers established New Amsterdam on Manhattan Island, and a few decades later, the English took over and renamed it New York setting the foundation for the thriving city we know today.
As the centuries passed, New York became a gateway of hope and opportunity. Millions of immigrants arrived through Ellis Island, bringing diverse cultures and dreams that shaped the city’s energy and identity. With the rise of landmarks like the Statue of Liberty, the Brooklyn Bridge, and early skyscrapers, New York grew into a global symbol of ambition, innovation, and possibility. Today, every street, museum, and neighborhood reflects the rich history that makes New York an unforgettable place to explore.

Travel Guide

Your Guide to Exploring New York

Planning a trip to New York? Whether you're exploring the excitement of New York City or discovering the natural beauty of upstate destinations, this travel guide will help you make the most of your visit. From transportation tips to the best times to go, here’s everything you need to know for a smooth and unforgettable experience.

Best Time to Visit

New York is a year-round destination, but spring (April–June) and fall (September–November) offer the best weather and the most beautiful scenery. Winter brings festive celebrations and snowy charm, while summer is perfect for outdoor attractions.

Getting Around

• New York City: Use the subway, buses, taxis, or rideshares. Walking is often the fastest way between nearby attractions.
• Upstate New York: Renting a car is highly recommended, especially for parks, mountains, and scenic drives.

Where to Stay

• NYC: Midtown for convenience, Times Square for lights and entertainment, Brooklyn for trendy neighborhoods, and Queens for budget-friendly options.
• Upstate: Consider cozy lodges, lakeside cabins, or boutique inns for relaxing nature trips.

Travel Tips

• Wear comfortable shoes—New York involves a lot of walking.
• Book tickets early for popular attractions like the Statue of Liberty, Broadway shows, or the Empire State Building.
• Try local favorites: New York pizza, bagels, food trucks, and international dishes from every culture.
• Always check the weather and be prepared for quick changes, especially in spring and fall.

Must-See Highlights

• NYC: Times Square, Central Park, Statue of Liberty, Broadway, museums, skyscraper views.
• Upstate: Niagara Falls, Adirondack Park, Letchworth State Park, Thousand Islands, Finger Lakes wineries.
