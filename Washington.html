Welcome to Washington

Washington offers a stunning combination of vibrant cities, breathtaking landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. From bustling Seattle to peaceful national parks, this state has something for every traveler.
Explore the iconic Space Needle, enjoy the beauty of Mount Rainier, wander charming waterfront towns, or kayak in scenic Puget Sound. Washington’s diversity and natural splendor delight visitors year-round.

Space Needle

The Space Needle is Seattle’s iconic observation tower offering 360-degree views of the city, Puget Sound, and nearby mountains.
Take a trip up to the observation deck, dine at the rotating restaurant, and capture stunning photos of skyline and nature.

Mount Rainier National Park

Dominated by an active volcano, this national park offers alpine meadows, glaciers, and dense forests for hiking, wildlife viewing, and camping.
Explore trails suited for all skill levels, enjoy stunning wildflower displays, and experience breathtaking mountain vistas.

Olympic National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring diverse ecosystems from temperate rainforests to rugged Pacific coastline.
Hike through rainforest trails, spot wildlife, and enjoy the breathtaking beaches and mountain peaks.

Pike Place Market

Established in 1907, this bustling market is famous for fresh seafood, local produce, artisan crafts, and the world’s first Starbucks.
Watch fishmongers toss salmon, explore eclectic shops, and savor delicious street food in a lively atmosphere.

San Juan Islands

A serene archipelago known for kayaking, whale watching, charming small towns, and natural beauty.
Discover secluded beaches, scenic cycling routes, and vibrant arts communities in this relaxing island getaway.

Museum of Pop Culture

A tribute to contemporary pop culture, featuring exhibits on music, film, video games, and science fiction.
Experience interactive displays, iconic memorabilia, and engaging performances in an architecturally unique space.

Leavenworth

A Bavarian-themed village nestled in the Cascades, known for festivals, outdoor recreation, and alpine charm.
Enjoy seasonal events, hiking trails, local cuisine, and picturesque mountain views in a fairy-tale setting.

Tacoma Museums

Tacoma hosts a rich variety of museums including the Museum of Glass, showcasing stunning glass art and design.
Visit contemporary art exhibitions, vibrant galleries, and engaging cultural experiences.

History

History of Washington

Washington's history is rich and diverse, shaped by Native American cultures, early European exploration, and rapid economic growth fueled by logging, fishing, trade, and technology.
Indigenous peoples inhabited the region for thousands of years before European settlers arrived. Washington became a U.S. territory in 1853 and a state in 1889. Its unique blend of indigenous heritage and pioneer spirit continues to influence the state's vibrant culture and dynamic economy today.

Travel Guide

Your Guide to Exploring Washington

Planning your trip to Washington? This guide offers travel tips, best times to visit, and advice to make the most of your experience.

Best Time to Visit

Spring through early fall (April to October) provides the best weather for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Getting Around

Use public transit in Seattle and other cities. Renting a car is recommended for exploring parks and rural areas.

Where to Stay

Stay in downtown Seattle for city life, or choose cozy lodges near national parks for nature immersion.

Travel Tips

  • Bring rain gear—it can be wet throughout the year.
  • Book popular tours and accommodations in advance during summer.
  • Try local seafood, especially salmon and shellfish.
  • Respect the natural environment and local cultures.

Must-See Highlights

  • Space Needle and Pike Place Market in Seattle.
  • Mount Rainier and Olympic National Parks.
  • San Juan Islands for wildlife and relaxation.
  • Bavarian charm of Leavenworth.
  • Tacoma’s vibrant arts and museums.
