Welcome to Texas

Texas is a vast blend of cultures, landscapes, and history with a strong spirit of independence. From dynamic cities to wide-open spaces, it offers something for every traveler.
Explore lively music scenes in Austin, enjoy BBQ feasts, visit historic missions, or roam deserts and prairies. Texas embraces tradition, innovation, and Southern hospitality.

The Alamo

The Alamo in San Antonio is a symbol of Texas’ fight for independence in 1836. Visitors can explore the historic mission, exhibits, and learn about the pivotal battle.
Experience guided tours and educational programs that reveal the courage and heritage behind this iconic site.

Big Bend National Park

Big Bend offers dramatic desert landscapes, rugged mountains, and the Rio Grande river. It’s perfect for hiking, camping, and spotting diverse wildlife.
Trek scenic trails, enjoy stargazing in one of the darkest night skies, and immerse yourself in wilderness adventure.

Space Center Houston

Official visitor center for NASA Johnson Space Center, offering interactive exhibits about space exploration.
Tour astronaut training facilities, see real spacecraft, and engage with hands-on science exhibits.

San Antonio River Walk

A vibrant network of walkways along the San Antonio River lined with shops, restaurants, and cultural sites.
Enjoy boat rides, lively festivals, and diverse cuisine in this picturesque urban oasis.

Dallas Arts District

The largest urban arts district in the U.S., home to museums, theaters, and galleries.
Discover world-class art collections, live performances, and architecturally stunning venues.

Guadalupe Mountains National Park

Known for Guadalupe Peak, the highest point in Texas, this park showcases rugged mountain terrain and desert flora.
Hike challenging trails, observe wildlife, and enjoy crisp mountain air in a peaceful setting.

Texas State Capitol

Located in Austin, the Texas State Capitol is a grand historic building rich in Texas political history.
Take guided tours to learn about Texas government and admire impressive architecture and art.

Padre Island National Seashore

A pristine barrier island with miles of sandy beaches, coastal dunes, and sea turtle nesting grounds.
Enjoy swimming, birdwatching, beachcombing, and exploring one of the longest undeveloped barrier islands in the world.

History

History of Texas

Texas has a rich and complex history shaped by its indigenous peoples, Spanish and Mexican rule, and its fight for independence and statehood.
Indigenous groups lived in Texas thousands of years before European settlers arrived. Spanish explorers claimed the land in the 16th century, establishing missions and settlements. Texas declared independence from Mexico in 1836 after a series of conflicts including the famous Battle of the Alamo and won independence following the Battle of San Jacinto.

Texas joined the United States in 1845, leading to the Mexican-American War that defined its southern border. The state played a key role in the Civil War and Reconstruction. Later, the discovery of oil propelled its economy, making Texas a powerhouse of energy production and innovation.

Today, Texas blends this pioneer spirit and diverse cultural heritage into a vibrant identity, influencing American culture and economy.

Travel Guide

Your Guide to Exploring Texas

Planning a trip to Texas? Explore vibrant cities, vast natural parks, and rich history with this guide to attractions, transportation, and travel tips.

Best Time to Visit

Texas offers year-round travel options, with spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) providing the most comfortable weather and outdoor-friendly conditions.

Getting Around

Major Cities: use public transit, taxis, and rideshares in Houston, Dallas, and Austin. Walking is convenient in city centers.
Outside Cities: renting a car is recommended for exploring parks, small towns, and wide-open landscapes.

Where to Stay

Urban: hotels in downtown Austin, Houston, and Dallas offer convenience and entertainment.
Nature: lodges near Big Bend or cabins in the Hill Country offer peaceful retreats.

Tips

  • Pack for varied weather, including sun protection and layers.
  • Book tours and accommodations early, especially during festivals and peak seasons.
  • Savor Tex-Mex cuisine, barbecue, and local craft beers.
  • Enjoy the friendly Texan hospitality and respect local customs.

Must-See Highlights

  • The historic Alamo and scenic San Antonio River Walk.
  • Expansive wilderness of Big Bend and Guadalupe Mountains National Parks.
  • Vibrant arts, music, and nightlife scenes in Austin and Dallas.
  • Space exploration attractions at NASA’s Houston Space Center.
  • Beautiful beaches and wildlife at Padre Island National Seashore.
