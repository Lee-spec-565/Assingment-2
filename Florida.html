Discover Florida

Welcome to Florida, the Sunshine State! Known for its stunning beaches, vibrant cities, and unique natural beauty, Florida offers endless adventures. From the world-famous theme parks to tranquil coastal resorts, experience the perfect blend of relaxation and excitement in this tropical paradise.
Relax on the beaches of Miami, explore the Everglades, visit Orlando's theme parks, or enjoy outdoor activities in the Florida Keys. Florida is a diverse destination full of fun, nature, and culture.

Walt Disney World Resort

Walt Disney World in Orlando is the most famous theme park complex, offering enchanting rides, entertainment, and character encounters. It’s a magical destination for families and Disney fans.
Experience thrilling rides, Disney Princess meet-and-greets, spectacular parades, and fireworks. Enjoy multiple parks, including Magic Kingdom, Epcot, and Disney’s Hollywood Studios.

Everglades National Park

The Everglades is a vast subtropical wilderness known for its unique ecosystems, alligators, and airboat tours. It’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a haven for wildlife.
Take an airboat ride through the marshes, observe rare bird species, and explore nature trails. It’s perfect for eco-adventures and birdwatching.

Miami Beach

Miami Beach is famous for its white sandy beaches, lively nightlife, and Art Deco architecture. It’s the place for sunbathing, water sports, and vibrant cultural festivals.
Relax on South Beach, walk the historic Ocean Drive, or indulge in waterfront dining. The city offers both relaxation and exciting nightlife.

Kennedy Space Center

The Kennedy Space Center showcases NASA’s space exploration achievements with interactive exhibits, astronaut encounters, and rocket displays.
Tour the launch pads, see real spacecraft, and learn about space science. A must-visit for space enthusiasts and curious travelers.

Key West

The southernmost point of the continental U.S., Key West is known for its relaxed vibe, coral reefs, and historic sites. It’s a tropical escape with beautiful sunsets and lively culture.
Snorkel the reefs, visit the Ernest Hemingway Home, or stroll along Duval Street for dining and nightlife.

Busch Gardens Tampa Bay

Busch Gardens Tampa Bay offers a thrilling mix of rides and extensive animal exhibits, combining adventure and wildlife experiences.
Enjoy roller coasters, live shows, and safari tours where you can see exotic animals up close. It’s perfect for families and thrill-seekers alike.

History

History of Florida

Florida’s history is a rich tapestry woven from Native American cultures, European exploration, and diverse colonial influences.
Indigenous peoples thrived in Florida for thousands of years before Spanish explorer Juan Ponce de León arrived in 1513, naming it "La Florida." Spain established one of the earliest European settlements in St. Augustine in 1565, which remains the oldest continuously inhabited city in the U.S.

Florida changed hands between Spain, Britain, and the United States multiple times before becoming a U.S. territory in 1821 and the 27th state in 1845. The state’s strategic location made it a key player in the Civil War and a hotspot for trade and tourism.

In the 20th century, Florida’s economy blossomed with the growth of agriculture, space exploration at Cape Canaveral, and a booming tourism industry highlighted by attractions like Walt Disney World.

Today, Florida’s multicultural heritage and dynamic growth continue to shape its vibrant culture and economy.

Travel Guide

Explore Florida

Planning your trip? Florida offers a variety of attractions year-round. Best visiting times are in spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) when the weather is pleasant.
Use rental cars for exploring beyond cities; fly into major airports like Miami, Orlando, or Tampa. Stay in beachfront resorts, city hotels, or cozy vacation rentals. Wear comfortable shoes, book popular attractions early, and enjoy local cuisine like seafood, Cuban sandwiches, and key lime pie.
